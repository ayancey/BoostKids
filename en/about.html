Target Population

Boost Kids! is open to students entering 1st 2nd to 3rd grade, targeting most needy kids in:

  • Belle Aire and John Muir Schools
    • Percent qualifying for free and reduced lunch: 62.2
    • Rating: 5 out of 10 (www.greatschools.org)
  • All other San Bruno schools

Registration Process

Teachers at Belle Air and John Muir recommend and refer students to Boost Kids! based on students' current classroom performance.

The criteria for referring a student include performance a year or more below grade level standard, as measured by current instructional reading levels and classroom teacher evaluation.

Students will be admitted into the program according to number of tutors available. Boost maintains a student-to-tutor ratio of approximately 4:1.

Registration begins in the spring of every year. There is a registration deadline for all families interested in the program. Once registration has been closed, priority guidelines will determine which children will be admitted into the program.

Students of Belle Air Elementary will be given first priority.

Number of Students Served

Summer Boost Kids! currently serves 30 students with 4 staff members and at least 2 volunteers in the classroom during the day.

Our goal is to serve 40 students during summer 2014.

The Belle Air and John Muir students are recommended for the program by their classroom teacher based on need and parent support.

Most of the students are low income. Fees are on a sliding scale, from as low as $15-$100 for the summer.

Staffing

Boost Kids! employs 4-6 paid tutors (a tutor-to-student ratio of 1:4). These tutors are college and high school students ages 16 to 18 and above.

Boost Kids! also employs one executive director and reading specialist teacher, and six or more volunteers (speakers, reading tutors, etc.).

Structure

Students are assessed prior to the start of the program. Once are assessed, his or her enrollment will be prioritized according to need. Students will rotate through the different “centers” every day for 20-30 minute intervals, in groups of 4-5 students.

Literacy centers are as follows:

  • Read Naturally – fluency
  • Phonics for Reading – phonics/phoneme segmentation
  • Guided Reading Group (4) – phonics/vocabulary/comprehension
  • Peer Assisted Learning Strategies – fluency/comprehension
